Blood Facts

Incredible Facts About Human Blood

Blood is far more complex and vital than most people realize. Here are some remarkable facts about this life-sustaining fluid.

üî¥ 25 Trillion
Red Blood Cells in Your Body

Your body contains roughly 25 trillion red blood cells (erythrocytes), each carrying hemoglobin to transport oxygen from lungs to every tissue and organ.

üíõ 55%
Blood is Mostly Plasma

Over half of your blood is plasma ‚Äî a yellowish liquid made of 92% water. It carries proteins, hormones, nutrients, and waste products throughout the body.

‚ö° 2 Seconds
Someone Needs Blood

Every 2 seconds somewhere in the world, a patient needs blood ‚Äî whether due to surgery, cancer treatment, trauma, or chronic illness like sickle cell disease.

üè• 35 Days
Whole Blood Shelf Life

Donated whole blood only lasts 35 days when refrigerated. Platelets expire in just 5‚Äì7 days. This is why continuous donations are critical to maintain supply.

üß¨ 4 Types
Main Blood Group System

The ABO blood group system categorises blood into 4 types: A, B, AB, and O ‚Äî determined by antigens on red blood cells. Combined with Rh factor, this gives 8 types.

üåç O+
Most Common Blood Type

O+ is the most common blood type, found in about 38% of people globally. O- is the universal donor type ‚Äî it can be given to patients of any blood type in emergencies.

What is the Rh Factor?

The Rh factor (+ or -) refers to whether your red blood cells carry a specific protein called the Rh antigen. If you have it, you're Rh positive (+). If not, you're Rh negative (-). Rh negative blood (especially O-) is critical in emergencies because it can be given to any patient before their blood type is confirmed.

Requirements

Weight at least 50 kg

Hemoglobin ‚â• 12.5 g/dL
